History of BMW

History of BMW, or Bayerische Motoren Werke, has a rich history that began in 1916. Initially, the company was engaged in the production of aircraft engines, but later switched to the production of motorcycles and cars. The first car under the BMW brand appeared in 1928, and since then the company has become one of the most famous manufacturers of premium cars in the world.

Design and innovation

BMW cars are known for their recognizable design, which combines sportiness and elegance. From aggressive body lines to refined interior details, every BMW vehicle exudes style and dynamism. The classic radiator grille, often called "nostrils", and modern LED headlights are integral design elements that give the cars a recognizable look.

  • 1917. BMW I IIA: the first aircraft engine with aluminum pistons.
  • 1935. BMW R 12 and BMW R 17: the first hydraulic front fork.
  • n 1935, at the German motor show, BMW presented not only two newly created models of motorcycles R 12 and R 17, but also a world innovation - a hydraulic damper on telescopic forks.
  • 1936. BMW 328: the most successful sports car of the late 1930s.
  • BMW 328 introduced innovative lightweight chassis and aluminum cylinder heads. The modernized silhouette of the car has improved aerodynamic qualities and external attractiveness.
  • 1954. BMW 502: the world's first mass-produced aluminum V8 engine.
  • At the Geneva International Motor Show in 1954, BMW introduced the 502 model with a 2.6-liter 8-cylinder aluminum alloy engine. With 100 hp. the car became the fastest on the market of tourist vehicles.
  • 1973. Construction of the BMW tower.
  • The new BMW headquarters was created as a high-rise building with four columns. Its unique design, construction and engineering solutions are still considered innovative forms of civil engineering. Since then, the building has been a historical monument. The BMW Group plant in Leipzig and BMW Welt continued the tradition of advanced architecture.
  • 2009: presentation of a sports car with a BMW Vision EfficientDynamics hybrid engine.
  • 2009: the new FIFTH BMW 550i sedan.
  • 2011: innovative BMW ActiveE electric car.
  • 2011: launch of innovative production of carbon fiber-reinforced superlight plastic for BMWi cars.
  • 2013: release of BMW i3 and BMW i8
